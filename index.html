Paper Abstract

This paper introduces CVSC, a real-time and temporally consistent semantic video communication approach. We incorporate the last available frame and motion information to reduce the number of diffusion steps to as low as four to enable real-time video synthesis. Additionally, we incorporate windowed temporal cross-attention to support temporal consistency. We evaluate our approach objectively and highlight the limitations of metrics used to evaluate generative models for semantic video communication. We further demonstrate the preferability of our approach under very low bitrates (< 0.006 bpp) in a subjective setting.

This supplementary webpage shows example videos from the validation set of the Cityscapes dataset at various bit rates. We compare our approach, i.e. CVSC with baseline approaches, namely OASIS, ControlVideo, and ControlVideo-4Step.
